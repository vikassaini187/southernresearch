Marketing

SPublic Relations at Southern Research is a deliberate, ethical, planned and sustained effort to establish and maintain mutual understanding between the organization and its publics. All corporate level and technical group level public relations, media relations and crisis communications efforts are led by the Public Relations Manager.Public Relations serves as the corporate spokesperson for Southern Research and is the primary point-of-contact for all media inquiries and other types of external requests for information about the company. A primary function of Public Relations is to further Southern Researchs interests through targeted media coverage of strategic messages and events. The Public Relations Office is adept at working angles in order to obtain free media coverage.

Media Relations, Crisis Communications and Social Networking Sites

Media relations of all types-including general media contact, media contact during a crisis situation, and/or representation of Southern Research on social networking sites or other Internet sites- is evaluated, authorized and monitored by the Public Relations Office. Generating positive media coverage about Southern Research is highly desirable, and reporters are anxious to work with the company to break those stories. Good news stories help to raise awareness and credibility about Southern Research in the communities and with the industries we serve. Good news stories also help enhance and protect the reputation of Southern Research and its employees.

Suggesting Public Relations Opportunities

Southern Research scientists, engineers, technicians and administrative support staff, are often on the front lines of novel research and new business ideas, and can be effective "reporters" of potential news opportunities. All employees are encouraged to notify the PR Manager of any "news" that could be of interest to people outside the organization or to other employees

News includes but is not limited to:
  • A scientific or engineering breakthrough or a new discovery
  • Publishing a scientific paper
  • Speaking engagement or presentation to your peers
  • Receiving an honor or award
  • Winning a big contract
  • Participating in a conference or meeting
  • New hires (manager or above)
  • Addition of new research capabilities or cutting-edge technology
  • Embarking upon a new area of research
Southern Research "Sources"

One of the best ways employees can assist Public Relations in generating media coverage is to serve as "sources" on topics that reporters are covering. This is especially important when there is national news breaking on a topic on which we have significant expertise, such as the NASA space program and emerging infectious diseases. Having a list of "sources" enables the PR Manager to make a quick connection between a reporter in search of a source, and scientists and engineers who can represent Southern Research in the matter.

To become a member of the Southern Research Sourcebook, please complete the Source Form, and submit it to the PR Manager by email. Marsha Hatfield
